Frequently Asked Questions

1. What is AQUA-MER aqueous speciation database?

AQUA-MER is the first online database focussing on Hg speciation in the environmental water systems. It collects experimental data, including solvation free energies and pKas, of low molecular weight organic/inorganic compounds in aqueous solution, and stability constants (log K) of these compounds interacting with Hg.

2. What can I do with AQUA-MER?

AQUA-MER provides online tools to calculate solvation free energies, pKas, and stability constants with ab initio quantum chemical methods, such as density functional theory (DFT). When the experimental data are not available, calculated constants can be applied to perform the Hg speciation (under construction).

3. Who is behind AQUA-MER?

AQUA-MER is run and devised by the Center for Molecular Biophysics (CMB) of the University of Tennessee/Oak Ridge National Laboratory (UT/ORNL). It is supported by an award from the Subsurface Biogeochemical Research (SBR) Program of the Biological and Environmental Research arm of the Office of Science of the U.S. Department of Energy (DOE).

4. How can I use AQUA-MER?

We have been trying hard to make AQUA-MER as easy as possible to use. If you have suggestions, please feel free to contact us.
