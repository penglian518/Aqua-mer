Frequently Asked Questions

1. What is AQUA-MER aqueous speciation database?

AQUA-MER is the first online database focus on Hg speciation in the environmental water systems. It collects experimental data, including solvation free energy, pKa of low molecular weight organic/inorganic compounds in aqueous phase, and stability constant (log K) of these compounds when interact with Hg.

2. What can I do with AQUA-MER?

AQUA-MER provides a bunch of online tools to calculate the solvation free energy, pKa, and stability constant with ab initio methods, such as density functional theory (DFT). When the experimental data are not available, calculated constants could be applied to perform the Hg speciation and do the modeling as well.

3. Who is behind AQUA-MER?

AQUA-MER is owned by Center for Molecular Biophysics (CMB) of University of Tennessee/Oak Ridge National Laboratory (UT/ORNL). It is supported by the Subsurface Biogeochemical Research (SBR) Program of Department of Energy (DOE).

We have been trying hard to make it as easier as possible to use. If you have good suggestions, please feel free to contact us.
