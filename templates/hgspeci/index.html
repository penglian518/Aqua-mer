Instruction on how to use the Speciation module

The Speciation module provides an intuitive online tool for researchers to study the speciation of given solutions. Although it integrate extensive parameters for Hg related complexes, it can also be applied to study the speciation of other elements, such as Fe, Cd, Co etc. It allows the researchers to study the solutions with their own parameters as well. Together with the online toolkit provided by AQUA-MER, the users can easily calculate the necessary parameters with quantum mechanics methods, such as DFT.

To use the Speciation module, the following three steps are required: 1, input the parameters; 2, review the input and submit the job; 3, view and download the results.
If you are familiar with AQUA-MER, you can start by clicking the "Input parameters" from the left menu. Otherwise, please read this instruction first.

Step 1. Input parameters

In this step, the user will build up a virtual solution which contains two parts, the Environmental Conditions and the Individual Element/species. Parameters in the Environmental Conditions section, such as Temperature, pH range, Concentration Unit etc. will be applied to the whole solution. Parameters in the Individual Element/species section will only affect the corresponding element/species and will override the parameters in the first section.

    a). Environmental Conditions


    b). Individual Element/species parameters (will provide related parameters in the database, and the option to add parameters by the users.)


Step 2. Review & submit

Before submission, you can review your input parameters. You can either go back to revise your input or proceed to submit the job to the server. The job ID will be shown on top of your input as well. With this job ID, you can close your webpage and come back to check the result later by clicking the "Results" from the left menu.

Step 3. View and download the results

Once the job is finished, you can either view and analysis your results online or download and plot them yourself.
